About Eric

Two markets. One operator. A long-term thesis.

I grew up in Burundi and now study International Business in Belgium. I sit at the intersection of two economies that should trade more efficiently, understand each other better, and lose less value at the border.

The Story

I was born in Burundi, where informal trade, scarcity, and resilience are part of daily economic reality. Moving to Belgium gave me access to the other side of the equation: structured markets, regulated commerce, logistics systems, and institutional capital.

That contrast is my operating advantage. I understand both the practical logic of African SMEs and the procedural logic of European systems.

My work is about reducing friction between those two worlds: less theory, more execution.
